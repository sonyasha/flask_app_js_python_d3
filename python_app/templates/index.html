Shots

The DC Metropolitan Police Department uses ShotSpotter to respond to instances of outdoor gunfire. ShotSpotter uses a series of audio-sensors and closed-circuit cameras to notify police of the locations of likely gunshots in real time. The data provided by ShotSpotter includes audio recordings of the sounds that are picked up, allowing officers to verify that these sounds are actual gunshots. Prior to ShotSpotter, approximately 80-90% of gunshots went undetected. Now, police are much more likely to know when a gun is fired and to be able to quickly respond to such incidents.
